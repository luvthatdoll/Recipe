Ultimate Chocolate Chip Cookies

Recipe by: Mik bentlay

Servings: 18 cookies

Equipment

  • Baking sheets
  • Parchment paper
  • Mixing bowls
  • Electric mixer

Ingredients

1¼ cups  All-purpose flour
3 tbsp  Cornstarch
½ tsp  Salt
½ tsp  Baking soda
½ cup  Cold butter (1 stick)
6 tbsp  Granulated sugar
¼ cup  Brown sugar (packed)
1  Large egg
½ tsp  Vanilla extract
2 tbsp  Fresh rosemary (finely chopped)
1 bag  Ghirardelli double chocolate chips
¼ cup  Walnut pieces (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C).
  2. Prepare baking sheets with parchment paper to prevent sticking.
  3. In the smaller mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, corn starch, salt, and baking soda.
  4. Cut the stick of butter into smaller pieces.
  5. In the larger mixing bowl, combine the butter, sugar, brown sugar, egg, and vanilla extract.
  6. Gently stir the dry ingredients into the larger bowl with the butter/egg/sugar mixture.
  7. Add in chocolate chips and optional walnuts. Mix gently until fully combined.
  8. Using a tablespoon, make cookie dough balls and place them on the baking sheet.
  9. Bake for 6-7 minutes or until the tops of the cookies get golden/brown.
  10. Remove from oven and let them cool (they'll continue cooking as they cool).
  11. For the softest cookies, eat them warm before they're totally cooled.

Recipe Tips

  • Don't overmix the dough - This will result in dry, crumbly cookies.
  • Don't overbake - Bake for just 6-7 minutes at 475°F for soft cookies (they'll continue cooking as they cool).
  • For extra-large cookies:
    • Use 2-tablespoon scoops (doubles the size)
    • Increase baking time to 9-10 minutes
    • Makes about 9 large cookies instead of 18
  • Optional add-ins - Try walnuts or pecans for extra crunch.
  • Serving tip - Eat while warm for the softest texture!
